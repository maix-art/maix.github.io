XR LATAM GRANT 2025 CALL

The XR LATAM Grant supports XR creatives throughout Latin America. We focus on projects and experiences that generate artistic expressions about current social realities and popular culture.
We seek examples of creative excellence, technical quality, and innovation through the hybridization of different media and disciplines, with special emphasis on the ethical dimension of the proposals.

WHO IT SUPPORTS

The goal of this initiative is to support and empower independent Latin American creators based in Latin American countries. We recognize Latin America as a multiplicity of nationalities, indigenous peoples, gender expressions, and other ethnicities and identities where we are all included.

WHAT IT FUNDS

Funding should be used to significantly boost the project, such as development, production, post-production, and distribution costs, hiring a conceptual artist, or creating a prototype. Projects at any stage of creation are eligible.

WHO WE ARE

MAIX is an initiative created by and for artists, creators, artisans, designers, communicators, and other Latin American professionals using XR technologies, rooted in and representing their communities. We believe in providing a caring space for collaboration and mutual learning where participants can form vital connections.
The goal of this initiative is to support and empower independent Latin American creators based in Latin America. We recognize the region as a diversity of nationalities, identities, Afro-descendant and indigenous peoples, gender expressions, and other ethnicities where we are all included.

MISSION

We foster spaces for co-creation, collaboration, experimentation, and the sharing of knowledge and tools with our communities. We seek partnerships for the long-term development of XR art to systematically transform the region‚Äôs creative ecosystem in an inclusive and diverse way.
In our processes we value diverse experiences and contributions. We aim to reduce structural and systemic inequalities in access to creative processes, technology use, and funding.
